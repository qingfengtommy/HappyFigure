Overall Aesthetic
The style is "High-Density Genomics Publication" (similar to Nature Genetics or Cell). It features a rigorous "Small Multiples" (or Trellis) layout, maximizing data density per square inch. The design is strictly 2D, minimalist, and uses a white background with no grid lines to maintain clarity across many subplots.
Color Palette
Use a muted, categorical palette specific to each column's function:
Column A (Grey/Blue):
Points/Lines: Dark Grey (#707070)
Shading (Confidence Interval): Muted Slate Blue (#A6Bad8 with 0.3 alpha)
Column B (Green/Purple/Blue):

Font Choices and Sizes
Typeface: Standard Sans-Serif (Helvetica or Arial).
Panel Tags (a, b, c, d): Bold, approx 12-14pt, aligned to top-left of the column.
Row Labels (Y-axis): Regular weight, approx 9pt. Note: Row labels are grouped on the far left with bracket lines indicating clusters (e.g., "Track prediction").
Axis Ticks: Small, approx 8pt.
Legend: Regular weight, small (8pt), placed at the very top of each column.
Line Weights and Marker Styles
Lines:
Primary Data Lines: Thin, often dashed (linestyle='--'), linewidth approx 1.0pt.
Fit Lines (Col A): Solid thin grey line.
Markers:
Size: Small (approx size 15-20 in Matplotlib).
Shapes: Distinct use of Circles (o), Triangles (^), and Plus signs (+) to distinguish series.
Intervals: Shaded regions (fill_between) are used for standard deviation/confidence, with high transparency and no outline.
Grid and Axis Styling
Spines: "L-frame" style. Only Left and Bottom spines are visible. Top and Right spines are removed (despine).
Grid: None. No horizontal or vertical grid lines inside the plots.
Ticks: Outward facing, black, short length.
X-Axis Labels:
Cols A-C: Log-scale or categorical spacing with labels only at the bottom row.
Col D: Categorical text labels rotated 45 degrees.
Layout Proportions
Grid Structure: A dense 12-row by 4-column grid.
Aspect Ratio: Individual subplots are wide and short rectangles (approx 3:1 aspect ratio).
Spacing:
Vertical spacing between rows is minimal to facilitate scanning down a column.
Horizontal spacing between columns is wider to separate the distinct experiments.
Grouping: The rows are visually grouped on the far left using thin bracket lines with vertical text labels (e.g., "Track prediction", "Variant effect prediction").
Design Specifics
Consistency: Y-axis limits vary per row but are consistent across columns where applicable.
Baselines: Dashed horizontal black lines indicate baselines (e.g., in Col D) or performance thresholds.figure5.png
